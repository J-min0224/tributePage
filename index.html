Hendrik Johannes Cruijff

Father of Modern Football
Cruyff is regarded as one of all-time greats and is a true Blaugrana legend, having worked wonders both as a player and as a coach during his time at the club.

    Here's a time line of Johan Cruijff's life

    25 Apr 1947 Born in Amsterdam,Netherland
    25 Apr 1957 Joined the Ajax youth team
    15 Nov 1964 Made his first team debut
    1964-1973 Ajax(318 appearances, 250 goals)
    2 Jun 1971 Won his first European Cup
    28 Dec 1971 Named the Dutch and European Footballer of the Year
    30 Aug 1973 Moved to Barcelona for $2 million (World Record Transfer Fee)
    1973-1978 Barcelona (184 appearances, 61 goals)
    14 Jul 1974 Led the Netherlands to a runners-up medal in the 1974 World Cup
    5 Jul 1980 Signed a lucrative deal with the LA Aztecs of the North American Soccer League(NASL)
    1978-1979 Los Angelas Aztecs (27 appearances, 14 goals)
    1979-1981 Washington Diplomats (32 appearances, 12 goals)
    1981 Levante (10appearances, 2 goals)
    1981-1983 Ajax (52 appearances, 20 goals)
    1983-1984 Feyenoord (44 appearances, 13 goals)
    13 May 1984 Announced his retirement, Decided to become a football coach
    25 Aug 1988 Returned to Barcelona as a coach of the first team
    24 Mar 2016 Died peacfully in Barcelona
